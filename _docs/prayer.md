## Collect
Almighty God, who taught the hearts of your faithful people by sending them the light of your Holy Spirit: grant to us the same Spirit to have a right judgement in all things and always to rejoice in his holy comfort; through the merits of Christ Jesus our Saviour, who lives and reigns with you and the Holy Spirit, one God, now and for ever. Amen

| **From the Passage** | Almighty God,<br>To you belong wisdom and power; counsel and understanding are yours. What you tear down cannot be rebuilt, and those you imprison cannot be released. If you withhold the waters, everything dries up; if you send them out, they overwhelm the land. In your hand is the life of every creature and the breath of all mankind.<br><br>Lord, we confess that like Job’s friends, we often look at suffering and create our own neat, cold explanations. We play the judge. We confess that as a society, we try to hide death away, pretending it is just a technical problem to be solved. Or we trivialize it, pretending it is nothing to fear.<br><br> But your word reminds us of the raw truth. Mortal man, born of woman, is of few days and full of trouble. We spring up like flowers and wither away; like fleeting shadows, we do not endure. We feel the touch of living death all around us—in our sickness, our anxieties, our aging bodies, and our grief. Like a rotten thing, we wear away, like a garment that is moth-eaten.<br><br> We admit that when suffering hits, we often want to flee. We treat death as an escape, wishing you would hide us in the grave and shelter us in secret until your anger passes. Yet we know there is nowhere to hide from your power. As mountains erode and crumble, and as stones are worn away by water, so you destroy our fragile human hope. You overpower us, and we pass away.<br><br>In our distress, we do not look for easy lectures or cheap answers. Like Job, our soul yearns for rescue. We want to be saved. Even if you slay us, yet we will hope in you. You alone are our salvation.<br>We thank you that we do not stand where Job stood, staring into the dark. We thank you that Jesus came into death itself. He became the cut flower, withered on the cross, so that death would be turned inside out and transformed into a means of life.<br>Because Jesus is risen, we pray that you would deliver us from the fear of death. Forgive us for gripping onto this life too tightly. Deliver us from trivializing the weight of eternity.<br><br>Fill our hearts with your love through the Holy Spirit. Free us to use our remaining days to love our neighbors, to serve the vulnerable, and to worship you without fear. We entrust our lives and our deaths to you, knowing that we are no longer our own, but belong to our faithful Savior, Jesus Christ.<br><br> Amen.|
