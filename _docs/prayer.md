## Collect
O God, the king of glory, you have exalted your only Son Jesus Christ with great triumph to your kingdom in heaven: leave us not desolate, but send your Holy Spirit to strengthen us, exalted us to where our Saviour Christ has gone before, who loves and reigns with you and the Holy Spirit, one God, for evermore. Amen.

| **From the Passage** | Lord God,<br> We come before You with Job, standing in the dust of a world that often feels chaotic and disproportionately cruel. We confess that we are often like Bildad—clinging to rigid, "safe" theologies because we are scared. Forgive us for using the Bible as a weapon to "fix" others rather than a place to meet You.<br> *Save us from being untrustworthy with the suffering of our neighbours*. Deliver us from simplistic answers and the urge to explain away what we cannot understand. Help us to stop "straw-manning" the pain of others just to protect our own sense of order.<br> *For those among us whose pain is beyond words:* We groan with them. Together, we pray with those crying out, "How long, O Lord?" We thank You that when we don’t know what to pray, Your Spirit intercedes for us with sighs too deep for language. We lean on Jesus—our great Mediator who knows the weight of unjustified suffering and who prays for us even now.<br> *Give us a "steel-man" faith.* Not a fragile faith that ignores reality, but a grit-filled trust that can lament and remain honest. We would rather be crushed by the Truth of who You are than comforted by a lie. Thank You for being a God strong enough to handle our questions and gentle enough to hold our hearts.<br> Amen.|
