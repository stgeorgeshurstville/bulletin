## Collect
Almighty and everlasting God, you have given us your servants grace by the confession of a true faith to acknowledge the glory of the eternal Trinity, and by your divine power to worship you as One: we humbly pray that you would keep us steadfast in this faith and evermore defend us from all adversities; through Christ our Lord. Amen.

| **From the Passage** |**Heavenly Father, God of Truth and Justice,**<br><br>We come before You acknowledging that faith is often forged in the dark. We thank You for the raw honesty of Scripture, and for the ancient testimony of Your servant Job. When his body was broken, his community had exiled him, and his friends relentlessly denied his reality, Job refused to buckle. He refused to accept a cheap, transactional version of You. We thank You for his stubborn, enduring faith—a faith that cried out from the ash heap, confident that his Creator was not his enemy, and that his Redeemer lived.<br><br>Lord, we confess that when the darkness closes in on our own lives, we so easily give in to despair. Forgive us for the times we judge Your character by our immediate, painful circumstances.<br><br>**Lord Jesus, the Author and Perfecter of our Faith**,<br><br>We see in Job the beautiful, prophetic template of Your own earthly walk. You entered our broken world and suffered the ultimate social exile. You were misunderstood by Your family, abandoned by Your closest friends, and falsely accused of blasphemy by the religious elite. They stripped You of Your humanity and hung You on a cross, declaring You to be cursed by God.<br><br>Yet, like Job, You held fast to the truth of the Father’s love. You committed Your spirit into His hands, trusting that the courtroom of heaven would have the final word.<br><br>And Father, how we praise You for the glorious dawn of Easter morning! Through the power of the resurrection, You shattered the darkness and completely validated the identity, the mission, and the faith of Your Son. The empty tomb is the cosmic verdict that proclaims to the universe: _Jesus is exactly who He said He was, and God is entirely for us, not against us_. **Holy Spirit, anchor our souls in this ultimate vindication**.<br><br>When we face suffering, isolation, or the mocking of a world that dismisses our faith, give us the courage of Job and the endurance of Christ. Remind us that we do not need to fight for our own reputations or lose sleep plotting revenge. Our vindication is already historically secure in the resurrection.<br><br>Help us to stand firm as witnesses to Your grace, knowing that the distress of this present time is temporary. Fill us with the joyful, unshakeable certainty that because our Redeemer lives, we too will rise. Keep our eyes fixed on that rapidly approaching day when the final stone will be rolled away from our suffering, and we will see You face-to-face, fully known and fully loved.<br>In the powerful, resurrected, and vindicated name of Jesus Christ,<br><br>Amen.|
