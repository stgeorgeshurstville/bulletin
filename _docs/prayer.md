## Collect
Almighty God, you alone can order the unruly wills and passions of sinful men: grant to your people that they may love what you command and desire what you promise, that so, among the many and varied changes of the world, our hearts may surely there be fixed where true joys are to be found; through Jesus Christ our Lord. Amen.

| **From the Passage** | Heavenly Father,<br> We come before You today with hearts that are often heavy and voices that sometimes struggle to find the right words. We thank You for the Book of Job—for the way it gives us permission to be honest, to be messy, and to be broken in Your presence.<br> Lord, we confess that there are seasons when the "light" feels like a burden, and like Job, we feel "hedged in" by trouble. For those among us sitting in the "wheelchair" of suffering—those wrestling with grief, mental exhaustion, or the wreckage of broken dreams—we ask for the courage to speak. Help us to remember that our honest lament is not a loss of faith, but a desperate reach for You.<br> Thank You, Lord, that when we wish to un-create our past or escape our present, You do not turn away in judgment. Instead, You meet us in the darkness. We praise You for Jesus, our Great Answerer, who took on the ultimate darkness of the Cross so that we would never have to face our trials alone.<br> When our pain impairs our view and we can only see a sliver of Your light, remind us that Your goodness remains constant. Even when we cannot see Your "smiling face," help us to trust the heart of the One who died for us.<br> Hold us fast in the turmoil. Be our peace in the restless nights. And lead us forward with the secure hope that because our Redeemer lives, the darkness is not the end of our story.<br> In the name of Jesus, our Hope and our Light, <br> Amen.|
