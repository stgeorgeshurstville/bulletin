### Job 14 (NIV)

 ¹ Mortals, born of woman,
  are of few days and full of trouble.
² They spring up like flowers and wither away;<br>
  like fleeting shadows, they do not endure.
³ Do you fix your eye on them?<br>
  Will you bring them before you for judgment?
⁴ Who can bring what is pure from the impure?<br>
  No one!
⁵ A person’s days are determined;<br>
  you have decreed the number of his months<br>
  and have set limits he cannot exceed.
⁶ So look away from him and let him alone,<br>
  till he has put in his time like a hired laborer.

⁷ “At least there is hope for a tree:<br>
  If it is cut down, it will sprout again,<br>
  and its new shoots will not fail.
⁸ Its roots may grow old in the ground<br>
  and its stump die in the soil,
⁹ yet at the scent of water it will bud<br>
  and put forth shoots like a plant.
¹⁰ But a man dies and is laid low;<br>
  he breathes his last and is no more.
¹¹ As the water of a lake dries up<br>
  or a riverbed becomes parched and dry,
¹² so he lies down and does not rise;<br>
  till the heavens are no more, people will not awake<br>
  or be roused from their sleep.

¹³ “If only you would hide me in the grave<br>
  and conceal me till your anger has passed!<br>
  If only you would set me a time<br>
  and then remember me!</p>
¹⁴ If someone dies, will they live again?<br>
  All the days of my hard service<br>
  I will wait for my renewal to come.
¹⁵ You will call and I will answer you;<br>
  you will long for the creature your hands have made.
¹⁶ Surely then you will count my steps<br>
  but not keep track of my sin.
¹⁷ My offenses will be sealed up in a bag;<br>
  you will cover over my sin.

¹⁸ “But as a mountain erodes and crumbles<br>
  and as a rock is moved from its place,
¹⁹ as water wears away stones<br>
  and torrents wash away the soil,<br>
  so you destroy a person’s hope.
²⁰ You overpower them once for all, and they are gone;<br>
  you change their countenance and send them away.
²¹ If their children are honored, they do not know it;<br>
  if their offspring are brought low, they do not see it.
²² They feel but the pain of their own bodies<br>
  and mourn only for themselves.”

## Sermon Outline <br>

- Theology of Death 
- Death as punishment 
- Death as mystery 
- Death that leads to Life
